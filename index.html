  1. Live preview Testnet app — email sign-in, simulated US/USDC marks, practice trade & earn UI.

  4. Token BALE — staking-only at launch: fee share, 12-month alignment lock, governance rights — no public LP vault on day one.

Investment highlights

  1. First dedicated cotton perpetual venue — vertical focus in a ~$45B global market.
  2. Disciplined launch: single pair liquidity before multi-origin sprawl.
  3. BALE aligns long-term investors with protocol fees and market expansion votes.
  4. Off-hours pricing IP (EMA + order-book fallback) built and running in the relayer today.

Problem

  • No efficient 24/7 hedging venue for cotton farmers, mills, and merchants.
  • Legacy ICE access is session-bound and operationally heavy for many participants.
  • Cross-origin basis risk (US, Brazil, India, Pakistan, Australia) remains structurally under-served.
  • DeFi commodity coverage is broad in narrative, narrow in execution.

Solution

  • US/USDC — ICE Cotton No. 2 perpetual, USDC collateral, 24/7.
  • Mark engine: external ICE when fresh; order-book EMA fallback when markets are closed.
  • Email-first onboarding — no MetaMask required for commercial users at testnet.
  • Origin perps (Brazil, Australia) architected; activation via governance after US liquidity proves out.

Market Opportunity

  • Global cotton market size: $44-45B in 2026.
  • Annual cotton production: around 120 million bales.
  • ICE cotton futures average daily notional: about $1.2B.
  • RWA in DeFi: $30-36B and growing rapidly.

Product mechanics (v1)

  • Single launch market: US/USDC on Arbitrum (Sepolia testnet now).
  • Reference mark: median(oracle, oracle + EMA(basis), book median) with per-tick clamps.
  • Collateral: USDC — familiar unit for mills, merchants, and funds.
  • Full app (wallet, on-chain settlement) — coming soon; preview is simulated.

Differentiation

  1. Category-defining venue: first dedicated cotton perpetual DEX.
  2. Commercial-user first design: real hedgers, not only speculative flow.
  3. Basis-risk aware architecture versus generic, single-index perp rails.
  4. Email sign-in and “Hedge My Crop” UX — built for operators, not only crypto natives.
  5. Governance-gated origin expansion — Brazil/Australia in testnet preview; mainnet via BALE vote.

Liquidity approach

  • Phase 1: protocol treasury seed + professional market makers — not retail LP at launch.
  • Target tight US/USDC spreads before opening additional origin books.
  • Fee rebates and industry partnerships before token emissions.
  • Optional LP vault evaluated only if MM + treasury depth is insufficient.

BALE token (planned)

  • Staking-only at launch — share of USDC trading fees, no public counterparty pool on day one.
  • 12-month lock for core stakers — long-term alignment with platform adoption.
  • Governance: fee parameters, treasury spend caps, new origin listings, β_const updates.
  • Token not live — design phase; no public sale on this site.

Roadmap

  • Now: testnet preview — marks, email sign-in, simulated trade & earn.
  • Next: Arbitrum Sepolia live testnet — contracts, US/USDC, hosted relayer.
  • Mainnet v1: US/USDC 24/7 + BALE staking & governance launch.
  • Post-launch: origin markets (Brazil, Australia, …) via governance proposals.
  • Later: fiat on-ramps, industry integrations, expanded commodity suite.

Risk & Governance Framework

  • Oracle + EMA fallback with staleness rules and impact-price (IPD) controls.
  • BALE governance timelocks on parameter and origin-listing votes.
  • Operational focus on market integrity, uptime, and collateral safety.

Why Now

  • Commodity participants increasingly require always-on risk transfer.
  • RWA adoption in DeFi has reached a scale where vertical specialization matters.
  • Basis-risk-aware product design creates a defensible edge over generic perps.
  • Stablecoin settlement rails now support global, continuous access.

Use of Proceeds

  • Product: US/USDC mainnet, relayer, email onboarding, on-chain settlement.
  • Liquidity: treasury MM seed and market-maker partnerships.
  • Distribution: commodity industry pilots (US first, origins via governance).
  • Risk & compliance: audits, insurance buffer, legal review for BALE.
