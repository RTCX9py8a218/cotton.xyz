Cotton through time

From the first harvest to the next chapter of open, global markets.

  1. Ancient ~5000 BCE

    First cultivation

    Cotton grown in the Indus Valley and coastal Peru — among humanity’s oldest domesticated crops.

  2. Ancient ~3000 BCE

    Ancient trade begins

    Egyptian and Indian cotton textiles traded across empires; cotton becomes a bridge between continents.

  3. Ancient ~500–900 CE

    Global diffusion

    Cotton knowledge spreads through the Islamic world to Spain and North Africa; weaving and trade routes mature.

  4. Medieval 1300s

    European expansion

    Cotton cloth imports reshape Mediterranean and European commerce; demand begins its long climb.

  5. Industrial 1793

    Industrial leap

    Eli Whitney’s cotton gin dramatically increases US production — cotton fuels an industrial age.

  6. Industrial 1840s–1860s

    Factory age

    Mechanized spinning and weaving in Britain and New England; cotton becomes a defining commodity of modern trade.

  7. Markets 1870

    Organized futures

    New York Cotton Exchange founded — benchmark pricing moves from local bargaining toward centralized markets.

  8. Markets 1870s

    Liverpool hub

    Liverpool emerges as the world’s cotton trading capital, connecting American growers to European mills.

  9. Modern 1970

    ICE Cotton futures

    Cotton futures consolidated on what becomes ICE Cotton No. 2 — today’s global benchmark for world cotton prices.

  10. Digital 1990s–2000s

    Electronic era

    Screen-based trading replaces open outcry; markets get faster but stay bound to session hours and institutional access.

  11. Today 2000s–2020s

    Access gap

    ~120 million bales produced annually; farmers in many regions remain far from transparent global pricing and hedging.

  12. Next 2026

    cotton.xyz

    Perpetual cotton markets on Arbitrum — ICE Oracle when live, Order Book when it is not — opening global access to benchmark pricing and risk tools, 24/7, for participants everywhere.
